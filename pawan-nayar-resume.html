Philosophy: The CMET Framework

At the heart of everything I build lies the CMET Framework. It's a relational grammar of my work; a living mesh where Content, Marketing, Experience, and Technology are not departments but dimensions of just one paradigm: user success. Each connection between them forms a current, carrying knowledge, emotion, and accessibility to millions. To me, this isn't strategy; it's physics, the human physics of how ideas travel and transform.

Content + Marketing (CM)

Content gives marketing narrative depth and credibility, making campaigns and connections more authentic and hyper-personalized.

Content + Experience (CE)

Content gives experience a soul; a story thread, transforming moments into meaning and memory. The experience must be individualized to final need of recipient user profiles.

Content + Technology (CT)

Content structures how technology delivers knowledge, focusing on user success, not just usage success.

Marketing + Experience (ME)

Marketing turns experiences into shareable journeys, bringing together audiences (individuals, teams, enterprises, governments) like never before.

Marketing + Technology (MT)

Marketing seeds tech innovation for speed, scale, and outreach; while driving new channels, analytics, and personalization at lower cost.

Experience + Technology (ET)

Experience guides technology to feel intuitive, transforming it from an engineer's product mindset into every user's celebration.

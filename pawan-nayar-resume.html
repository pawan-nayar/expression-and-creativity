Origin Story: Energy Conserved is Energy Generated

NAFEN National Prize Winner • 1988

At 17, while still in school, won the National Foundation of Indian Engineers (NAFEN) essay competition with a prescient vision of energy futures. Writing in the pre-internet era with limited data access, this teenage essay predicted solar energy dominance, fusion reactors, and space-based energy collection - technologies that would define the 21st century.

📄 Original Essay
🔮 What the 17-Year-Old Predicted vs. What Actually Happened
✅ Predictions That Came True
  • Solar Energy Dominance: "Solar energy would be the answer to the solution of energy crisis" - Solar became the fastest-growing energy source globally
  • Space-Based Solar: Predicted orbital solar collection - Now actively researched by NASA, Japan, and China
  • Fusion Reactors: Called them "manmade suns" - NIF achieved fusion ignition in 2022
  • Ocean Thermal Energy: Described temperature differential systems - Now commercially deployed
  • Geothermal Expansion: Predicted widespread adoption - Now mainstream renewable technology
📊 Data Points That Evolved
  • Oil Depletion Timeline: Predicted 28 years (by 2016) - New extraction technologies extended reserves
  • Energy Crisis Form: Expected physical scarcity - Got digital energy consumption crisis instead
  • Conservation Focus: Wrote about electrical waste - Now includes computational and attention energy
  • Information Access: Limited to state-sponsored data - Internet democratized energy information

💭 Energy Conserved is Energy Generated — A Conversation Across 36 Years

A quiet desk under a tube light in 1988 fades into the soft glow of a laptop in 2025.

Ink becomes pixels. Thought becomes data.

And two Pawans meet — one writing his first essay, the other reflecting on it.

1988 — The Boy

"Energy is the capacity to do work," I begin to write, gripping the pen too tight. The paper trembles slightly, as if it knows this is important. We've been told to save electricity, to switch off fans, to turn off lights. Our teachers say that the world will run out of oil by the time I grow up. So I write about solar energy, fusion, and the manmade sun — not because I've seen them, but because I believe they will save us.

I don't know much. Only what the newspaper prints, what the radio says, what the textbook repeats. But I know one thing: waste is wrong.

2025 — The Man

You were right, little one — but not in the way you thought. The world didn't end. We didn't run out of oil. We ran into new worlds — of data, code, and attention.

The energy crisis changed form. It hid itself inside our devices, our servers, our endless scrolling. Now, "saving energy" means something more subtle — saving our focus, our computation, our sanity.

The sentence you wrote — Energy conserved is energy generated — has become a new moral law for our digital civilization.

1988 — The Boy

But how? How can thinking waste energy? We used to light one bulb per room, and I felt guilty. Now you tell me that my thoughts can waste power?

2025 — The Man

Yes, in a way. Every redundant thread, every hallucinated AI response, every unnecessary simulation — all of it consumes invisible electricity. We built giant brains called Large Neural Networks. They reason, imagine, and generate — but they also devour power.

So your essay wasn't wrong — it was prematurely right. The new pollution is not just smoke and smog — it's unoptimized computation, unnecessary chatter, digital entropy.

1988 — The Boy

That's wild. I thought my essay was about switches and turbines, not thoughts and servers. Did people stop wasting?

2025 — The Man

Not yet. But we've learned something you didn't know then: the world doesn't move by one vector. When one path narrows, another opens. We found new fuels, then new minds, and now — new meanings.

Your essay predicted the end of energy; mine watches the evolution of energy — from physical to mental to digital. Back then, data was scarce. Now, data is infinite — and meaning is the new scarce resource.

1988 — The Boy

So do we still need my slogan?

2025 — The Man

More than ever. Because "energy conserved" now means attention saved, computation optimized, emotion redirected, and time reclaimed. You wrote it as physics. I live it as philosophy.

Together

Energy conserved is energy generated. A law of physics. A principle of civilization. And a whisper between two Pawans — one who dreamed, and one who understands.
